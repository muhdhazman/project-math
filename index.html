🌾 The Bioscience Problem

In Malaysian precision agrotech, tracking the Fresh Fruit Bunch (FFB) accumulation profile is key to optimizing fertilizer application and harvesting schedules.

While the total crop mass $f(x)$ tells us current yield, its derivative $f'(x)$ tells us the velocity of growth (tonnes/hectare/month). Pinpointing exactly when this rate peaks allows planters to maximize logistics and resource allocation before the growth rate plateaus.

🧮 Mathematical Model

Based on typical growth dynamics from the Malaysian Palm Oil Board (MPOB) Yield Performance Reports over a 24-month localized cycle.

Cumulative FFB Weight Formula:

f(x) = -0.003x³ + 0.12x² + 0.2x

Where x = months, f(x) = Yield (tonnes/ha)
Rate of Change Derivative:

f'(x) = -0.009x² + 0.24x + 0.2

Calculated via Power Rule: d/dx [xⁿ] = n·xⁿ⁻¹
